Credit Cards

When not chasing a credit card signup bonus, these are the cards I regularly use. None of these below have an annual fee and all offer straight cash back. Higher cash back/travel points are available from cards that do have annual fees.

Alliant 2.5% cash back

The best card for Costco/Walmart. All-purpose back-up card. Takes a month or two to setup (initial rate is only 1.5% cash back) with their checking account. Although, you might as well go for their other $300 bank account signup bonuses (use code: FASTPASS). You can get up to $400 but you might want to pass on the $100 for parking $10k. For more info, see DoctorOfCredit.

Juno 5% Debit Card

Debit card from a new online-only bank. Limited to 10 pre-selected merchants out of a relatively limited list. Able to change merchants at start of each month. For more info about the decent savings interest, see our post about the Juno bank account.

Citi Custom Cash 5% in select category

Unlike other 5% cards, you don't need to do extra work for this card. Whichever category you spent the most will be selected. Supermarkets is usually a good choice. You can also consider buying a physical gift card (Best Buy, Home Depot/Lowes, Amazon, etc.) from a major supermarket.

Chase Freedom / Discover, 5% in rotating categories

These 2 cards feature an ever-changing calendar that requires you to signup online a month before every quarter.

Capital One, 3% restaurant

Savor One. Although other cards also offer 3% for restaurant.
